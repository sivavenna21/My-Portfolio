  4. IoT Based Continuous Abiotic Factor Monitoring And Stastistical Analysis

    01/2022 — 05/2023

    Fundamental elements affecting human health and wellbeing include the quality of the air and water. However, current conditions have led to widespread contamination of these vital resources with toxins, which has resulted in a variety of health problems, including cancer, birth deformities, and respiratory ailments. Due to the high reactivity of the industrial chemicals, the issue is made worse in industrial settings where pollutants in the air and water can have a substantial influence on manufacturing operations. This study tries to address this problem by using IoT sensors to track the quality of the air and water in industrial settings. Real-time measurements of water quality like pH, turbidity, and temperature will also be taken, along with information on a variety of pollutants including particulate matter, ozone, and nitrogen dioxide. A public website is used to disseminate the acquired data after thorough analysis. The industry can better comprehend the possible effects on the environment's air and water quality as well as on the chemicals used in their operations by making this information public. They may take preventative action to lessen pollution and enhance the quality of the air and water with the aid of this analysis. The initiative also serves the general population and goes beyond industrial uses. People may learn about the air and water quality in their neighbourhood and take the required precautions to safeguard their health by sharing sensor data via a website.
